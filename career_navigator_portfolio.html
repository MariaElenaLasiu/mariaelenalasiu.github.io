01 — Problem

A valuable product that was asking too much, too soon

Whiteshield's Career Navigator is a Labour Market Intelligence platform built to give citizens data-driven career guidance. At the backend, it feeds real-time skills and employment data into Whiteshield's Quantum Governance model — informing national-level policy decisions. The product vision was compelling. The conversion reality was not.

🚧

The diagnostic-first trap

Users had to upload CVs, complete attitude surveys, and share detailed skills data — all before receiving a single insight. The product asked for trust before it had earned it.

📉

High drop-off at first contact

Only the most motivated users pushed through the upfront friction. Everyone else bounced — taking their data with them and leaving the Quantum Model starved of the signals it needed.

🕳️

Sparse data even from converters

Users who did convert shared the minimum viable data to access the tool — not enough to generate the rich "Individual Meaning" signals required for genuine policy intelligence.

🔇

No organic growth mechanism

The platform had no built-in virality. Every new user required direct acquisition effort — making scale expensive and slow.

⚠️ The core tension

The product needed high-fidelity data to deliver personalised value. But it needed to deliver personalised value first, to earn the right to collect high-fidelity data. This circular dependency was the structural friction to solve.

🎯 Strategic question

How do you increase both the volume and fidelity of citizen data — without a full platform rebuild and without sacrificing user trust?

02 — Process

How I diagnosed the gap and found the lever

This was a 2-week self-initiated sprint. No internal access, no stakeholder buy-in, no existing data. My job was to identify the strategic lever from the outside — using product analysis, user psychology, and labour market signals.

Step 1
Problem

Mapping the friction — where users were dropping off and why

I mapped the existing product journey end-to-end, identifying the specific moment where conversion collapsed: the upfront ask. Before users saw a single output, they were expected to provide CV data, complete self-assessments, and implicitly trust an unknown platform with sensitive professional information. I framed this as a value exchange imbalance: the platform was withdrawing trust before depositing value.

Step 2
Decision

Defining who to design for — and why this user, not all users

Rather than designing for a generic "career seeker," I synthesised labour market displacement research and behavioural signals to identify the highest-intent, highest-value segment. The insight: the most motivated users are not the most desperate — they are the most informed and most anxious. Mid-career professionals in AI-exposed sectors who understand the stakes but lack a clear action path. This informed anxiety is the product's entry point.

Step 3
Action

Reframing the value proposition — from assessment to agency

I audited the existing product narrative and identified a fundamental mismatch: the platform spoke the language of diagnosis, but users arrived in the language of urgency. I redesigned the value proposition around a single pivot: from "understand where you are" to "see your risk and get your roadmap." This shift required no new features — only a change in what the product leads with.

"Before: Upload your CV and we'll assess your skills. After: See your career risk score compared to national AI trends — and get a personalised roadmap to resilience."

Step 4
Impact

Designing the acquisition funnel — three features, one progressive journey

I translated the strategic reframe into three concrete feature interventions, each designed as an overlay on the existing platform. Together, they form a progressive value loop: Awareness → Engagement → Advocacy. Each step incentivises users to voluntarily share richer data in exchange for more personalised insights — flipping the data collection model from extraction to exchange.

03 — Solution

Three features. One progressive value loop.

Each feature was designed to be deployed as an overlay on the existing platform — requiring minimal engineering investment while maximising behavioural impact at the three critical moments of the user journey.

Designed for this user

👩‍💼
The "Informed Anxious" Professional
Mid-level · 3–7 years experience · AI-exposed sector
📰

Follows AI displacement news closely — knows enough to be worried, not enough to act with confidence.

🎯

Wants personalised, objective direction for their next skill investment — not generic trend data.

🔒

Cautious about sharing professional data with unfamiliar platforms; needs a clear value exchange first.

📣

Actively manages their LinkedIn presence and responds to verifiable signals of credibility.

1
Awareness · Zero friction

Resilience Index — "Your career risk in 30 seconds"

A high-impact landing-page widget that converts a user's job title into a personalised AI displacement risk score — benchmarked against national trends. No login, no upload, no commitment required. Users get immediate, specific, actionable data the moment they arrive.

🎯 Reduces bounce rate ⚡ Instant time-to-value 🧠 Triggers informed anxiety → action intent
2
Engagement · CV upload moment

Resilience Boost Roadmap — "Gap-to-Growth" visual

Immediately after CV upload, the platform generates a personalised "Gap-to-Growth" roadmap — showing exactly which 3 skills will increase the user's market value most, ranked by ROI. This transforms the CV upload from a data-extraction event into a value-delivery moment. The richer the CV, the richer the output — incentivising completeness.

📄 Drives CV upload volume 🔬 High-fidelity skill data for Quantum Model 🔄 Completeness-incentivised data loop
3
Advocacy · PLG loop

Future-Ready Credential — co-branded verifiable badge

Users who complete the roadmap receive a "Certified Future-Ready Skills" badge — co-branded by Whiteshield and the client government or ministry — shareable directly to LinkedIn. The credential serves as professional recognition and simultaneously creates a Product-Led Growth loop: every share drives organic acquisition from the exact same high-intent professional demographic.

📣 Organic PLG loop ↓ CAC via referral acquisition 🏛️ Institutional credibility signal

💡 The underlying logic

Each feature gives before it asks. Users progress from a 30-second anonymous interaction → a personalised roadmap → a shareable credential. At every step, they voluntarily share richer data in exchange for a proportionally more valuable output. The Quantum Governance model receives higher-fidelity signals at higher volume — without a single coercive data request.

How I chose these interventions over alternatives

Each feature was selected over plausible alternatives based on impact-to-build-cost ratio and alignment with the target persona's psychology.

04 — Impact

What success looks like — and how to measure it

Each KPI was selected because it ties directly to both user outcome quality and business sustainability. Metrics that could be gamed without delivering value were explicitly excluded.

📈

CV Conversion Rate

CTR from landing page to CV upload prompt. Measures how effectively the Resilience Index breaks first-contact friction. A rising rate signals the value proposition reframe is working — not just that traffic increased.

⏱️

Time-to-Value (TTV)

Time between first visit and first actionable insight delivered. Lower TTV directly correlates with higher retention and higher word-of-mouth advocacy — the two levers that reduce long-term CAC.

🧬

Data Integrity Score

Average number of unique skill-tags extracted per CV via the Quantum Engine. This is the proxy for policy-grade data quality — and the north star metric for Whiteshield's institutional mission.

🔗

K-Factor (Virality)

New signups attributed to organic LinkedIn referral links from credential shares. Measures PLG loop effectiveness and directly reduces CAC by replacing paid acquisition with peer-trust-driven referrals from the exact target demographic.

⚙️ Constraints this solution worked within

No internal platform access or user data. No stakeholder buy-in required. All three features were designed as platform overlays — deployable without core infrastructure changes. The 2-week sprint constraint forced prioritisation of the single highest-leverage intervention at each funnel stage rather than a comprehensive redesign.

05 — Reflection

What I'd validate next — and what this sprint proved

✅ The decision I'd protect

The choice to design for a specific high-intent persona rather than a broad "career seeker." The "Informed Anxious Professional" framing was the key that unlocked the right product language, the right moment of intervention, and the right organic growth mechanism. A generic approach would have produced generic features.

🔄 What I'd validate if I had access

I'd run a 5-participant moderated usability test on the Resilience Index landing widget to validate whether the risk-score framing triggers action intent or fatalism in real users — a critical unknown. I'd also run a structured expert interview with one Whiteshield policy analyst to stress-test whether the Data Integrity Score proxy actually maps to Quantum Governance signal quality.

💡 The assumption I'm making explicit

This sprint assumes that "informed anxiety" is the dominant motivational state of the target segment. That assumption was synthesised from labour market research — not validated through primary user interviews. If I'm wrong about the emotional trigger, the value proposition reframe loses its force. That's the first thing I'd test with a real user cohort.

06 — About this project

A self-initiated sprint to demonstrate product judgment

Career Navigator is a conceptual product strategy exploration — completed independently in 2 weeks, with no internal access, existing data, or stakeholder involvement. It was designed to demonstrate one specific capability: the ability to identify structural friction in a real product, reframe the value proposition using behavioural insight, and propose high-leverage interventions without requiring a platform rebuild.

It is a smaller and more constrained exercise than full-scale validation programmes like SugarWise. But it reflects the same discipline: start with the user's real problem, design around their natural behaviour, and measure success by outcomes that matter — not just metrics that move.
